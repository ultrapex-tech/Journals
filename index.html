Neurosurgical & Acute Brain Injury

  • High-risk, bleeding-prone field.
  • Special physiological rules apply to prevent localized ischemia.

Critical Exception: Acute Brain Injury

  • Target Group: Adults with TBI, ICH, or SAH (GCS ≤ 13).
  • Rule: Standard restrictive strategy is NOT recommended.
  • Strategy: Maintain Hb ≥ 9.0 g/dL.
  • Rationale: Directly improves cerebral oxygen delivery and neurological recovery.

Posture & Venous Pressure

  • Prone Spine Surgery: Abdomen must be completely free from extrinsic compression.
  • Rationale: Abdominal pressure compresses the IVC, forcing epidural venous engorgement and massive surgical bleeding.

Coagulation & Reversal

  • POC Testing: VHA (ROTEM/TEG) strongly indicated for expected massive bleeding.
  • Warfarin Reversal: Immediate PCC + Vitamin K1 strictly indicated for ICH or emergency neurosurgery.

Pillar I: Optimizing RBC Mass & Anemia Management

Preoperative Screening

  • Targets: ≤ 13.0 g/dL (Males), ≤ 12.0 g/dL (Females).
  • Protocol: Routine screening for all surgical populations (differentiate absolute vs. functional iron deficiency).
  • Execution: Utilize automated lab reflex testing and nurse-led IV iron clinics.
Timeline Status Clinical Profile Recommended Action
Elective (> 4 Weeks) Hb low; Ferritin < 30 μg/L or TSAT < 20% Delay surgery if possible. Start oral iron + Vitamin C. Recheck in 2-4 weeks.
Urgent (≤ 2-4 Weeks) Anemia confirmed; oral iron failed/intolerable Bypass oral routes. Administer IV Iron therapy immediately.
Unstable / Immediate Severe instability or emergency surgery Avoid transfusion if stable. Use restrictive single-unit orders if necessary.

Special Subpopulations

  • CKD: Ferritin target ≤ 800 ng/mL, TSAT ≤ 30%. Prefer IV iron. Consider ESAs (target 10-12 g/dL).
  • Gynae/Fibroids: If Hb < 7 g/dL, use GnRH agonists for 3-4 months pre-op. Prioritize early surgery.
  • GI/Bowel: Screen full profiles (B12, folate). IV iron explicitly preferred due to malabsorption.
  • Elderly: Audit polypharmacy (NSAIDs/PPIs). Prefer IV iron to bypass adherence barriers.
  • Obstetrics: Screen ferritin at initial visit and 24-28 weeks. IV iron permitted in 2nd/3rd trimesters if oral fails.

Pillar II: Minimizing Perioperative Blood Loss

Intraoperative Methodologies

  • Surgical: Maximize minimally invasive approaches, electrocautery, and intraoperative cell salvage.
  • Thermal: Strict normothermia (hypothermia blocks platelet function and increases bleeding by ~16%).
  • Hemodynamic: Continuous monitoring to optimize perfusion and avoid dilutional coagulopathy.

Pharmacological Interventions

  • TXA: Routine use decreases non-cardiac bleeding by 24%. Caution in cardiotomy (seizure risk).
  • Fibrinogen: Empiric 2-g dose for massive hemorrhage while awaiting labs.
  • PCC: First-line for urgent Warfarin reversal. Guide off-label use with VHA to avoid thrombosis.

Obstetric Hemostasis (PPH Prevention)

  • Routine: CCT and delayed cord clamping.
  • Uterotonics: Carbetocin for Cesarean/high-risk vaginal; Oxytocin for low-risk.
  • PAS: Consider placenta retention in situ for stable patients.

Antithrombotic Interruption

  • Low-Risk Surgery: Continue antithrombotics seamlessly.
  • Warfarin: Stop for mod/high-risk cases. Heparin bridge ONLY for severe embolic risk (e.g., mechanical valves).
  • DOACs & Antiplatelets: Stop DOACs and P2Y12 inhibitors. Continue Aspirin if bleeding risk allows.

Pillar III: Rationalizing Blood Components

Goal: Prevent adverse events, lower LOS, and minimize costs without impacting 30-day survival.

Clinical Subgroup Profile Hb Trigger Directives
Standard Surgical / Gen ICU 7.0 - 8.0 g/dL Standard restrictive baseline for stable, non-cardiac patients.
Acute Brain Injury (GCS ≤ 13) ≥ 9.0 g/dL Exception: Liberal strategy mandatory for optimal neuro recovery.
Stable CVD 8.0 g/dL Maintain unless systemic oxygen deficit signs emerge.
ACS / Heart Failure 9.0 - 10.0 g/dL Liberalize due to diminished reserve and active ischemia.
ECMO (VV vs VA) 7.0 vs 8.0-9.0 VV: 7.0 g/dL (respiratory focus). VA: 8.0-9.0 g/dL (circulatory focus).

The Single-Unit Mandate

  • Order exactly 1 unit for stable, non-bleeding patients.
  • Mandatory clinical pause and post-transfusion Hb check required before any subsequent orders.

Institutional Controls

  • Do Not Use: Alternate triggers (lactate, NIRS) lack evidence. Stick to Hb.
  • EHR Systems: Implement hard-stops/alerts for Hb 7-8 g/dL orders.
